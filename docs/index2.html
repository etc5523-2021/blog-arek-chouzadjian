Blog Post 2 - Spain and COVID-19

By Arek Chouzadjian

Introduction

This blog post seeks to examine two aspects of the response to the COVID-19 pandemic in Spain - vaccination and public health measures. First, Spains vaccination rate is compared to other large European nations, in terms of the overall percentage of those fully vaccinated, and the time at which vaccination rates peaked. Second, the timing of the imposition of public health measures in Spain is compared to the occurrence of spikes in case numbers, and Spains public health responses are compared to those of its European neighbours.

Data Story 1: Vaccinations

The first part of this analysis looks at vaccination rates in Spain compared to its large European neighbours. This involves looking at how full vaccination rates have increased over time, and how the rate of vaccinations per million people have fluctuated daily during 2021.

Plot 1: Total full vaccination rate comparison over time

This plot shows that, as of September 2021, Spain is the standout performer among the largest European nations in terms of fully vaccinating its population against COVID-19.

Like the other large EU countries, Spains vaccination program was initially slower than the UKs; for instance, by the beginning of June 2021, the UK had fully vaccinated ~40% of its population, compared to ~20% for Spain, France, Italy and Germany. However, Spains vaccination program has now raced past the UKs, as well as exceeding those of the other large EU nations. Spains full vaccination rate surpassed the UKs around late July 2021, and now sits at 75% of the population, which is ~11% higher than the next best-performing country, the UK (64%).

Plot 2: New vaccinations per million (smoothed) by country over time

This plot also shows how Spains vaccination program surged to become arguably the best performing in Europe. In the first months of 2021, Spains vaccination program, along with that of its EU contemporaries, was much slower than the UKs; in February 2021, the UK was vaccinating at more than 4 times the rate of the large EU nations.

However, after May 2021, Spains rate of new vaccinations surpassed the UKs, and peaked on 4 July 2021 at 12,363 vaccinations per million, far more than the UKs peak of 8,830 vaccinations per million on 31 March 2021, and more than Germanys (10,419, 14 June), Frances (10,297, 21 July), and Italys (9,814, 10 June). The result is that Spain now has the most highly vaccinated population out of the large European nations.

Overall, it can be seen that, after a relatively slow start when compared to the United Kingdoms feted vaccine roll-out, Spains full vaccination level against COVID-19 is among the highest in Europe, and in the world more widely. This holds Spain in better stead than many nations, at this time, to reduce hospitalisations and deaths from COVID-19.

Data Story 2: Public health measures

The aim of this section of the analysis is to see at what level of infections Spain increased the stringency of its public health measures, how that varied between 2020 and 2021, and how Spains public health measure stringency compared to its large European neighbours.

It must be noted that I excluded the first few months of the pandemic from the first plot, as the first wave of infections in Spain was largely not recorded, due to a lack of testing.

Plot 1: Comparison of infection levels per million to stringency of public health measures

To explain this plot, it is necessary to first explain how the measure of stringency of public health restrictions is calculated:

Stringency index

The Stringency index is a composite measure of nine metrics that relate to restrictions on civil society imposed to control the COVID-19 pandemic. The higher the index score, the more restrictive the measures. The nine metrics include: school closures, workplace closures, cancellation of public events, restrictions on public gatherings, closures of public transport, stay-at-home requirements, public information campaigns, restrictions on internal movements, and international travel controls. Different combinations of these can effect the index in different ways - the index score is a rough guide as to how restrictive all the measures collectively are on society.

The plot shows that, at the beginning of the time period, Spain was exiting a period of very strict public health measures. This was at the beginning of the pandemic, when there was the first wave of COVID-19 infections, but these were largely not recorded due to a lack of testing. Since then, Spain has experienced three other waves of infection, with peaks in November 2020, January 2021, and July 2021. In the case of the first two of these waves, the Spanish government increased the stringency of public health measures, although not to the same level as the first wave.

What stands out is the response to the most recent wave of infections. The Spanish government started to significantly ease restrictions from early May 2021; by the beginning of July, cases were rising sharply. However, the Spanish government did not impose any significant new restrictions in response to this. The reason for this is vaccination; the Spanish population has reached a level of full vaccination where the health system can cope with much larger numbers of cases, as not as many people are being hospitalised with severe illness.

Public health measure stringency comparison between countries:

This plot shows that Spain, in comparison to other large European nations, has not been any more or less prone to imposing strict public health measures to control COVID case numbers.

In the initial phase of the COVID-19 pandemic, beginning in February/March 2020, all of the large European nations imposed very strict public health measures to protect their health systems. Spain enacted its harshest period of restrictions from 30 March 2020 to 5 May 2020. The Spanish government then reduced restrictions to a degree during winter and autumn, but reimposed relatively harsh restrictions at the end of October 2020 (although not to the same level as March-May 2020). Restrictions in Spain were not significantly eased until May 2021, and reached their current level around mid-June (with some small easing after).

Spains experience is fairly typical of large European nations in general - all five have followed a relatively similar path. What is interesting to see is how the nations are responding in different ways to case levels in 2021, given that all of them have fully vaccinated at least 60% of their population:

  • Spain and the UK have eased restrictions very significantly, to the point where present restrictions are equivalent in stringency to those imposed just before the first national lockdowns in March 2020 (those restrictions were relatively light). Spain and the UK have the highest full vaccination rates of the countries examined here.

  • Germany is maintaining significantly tighter restrictions, tighter than those during the summer of 2020.

  • France and Italy had eased restrictions significantly, but have recently tightened them again to a certain extent.

Overall, Spain has followed a similar path to its neighbours in terms of imposing public health measures to protect the health system, and the population more widely. However, as of September 2021, there is evidence that Spains particularly high vaccination rate may be allowing it to eschew restrictions to a greater degree than some other European nations.
